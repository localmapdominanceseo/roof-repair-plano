Types of Roof Repair We Perform

Roof Leak Repair in Plano TX

Active roof leaks are our most urgent call type. Whether you're seeing water stains on your ceiling, drips during rain, or wet insulation in your attic, we locate the source and seal it fast. Plano leak repairs most commonly involve failed flashing around chimneys and skylights, cracked pipe boots, or damaged shingles at valleys and ridges. We don't just patch the visible spot — we trace the leak to its origin point, which is often several feet from where the water appears inside.

Shingle Roof Repair Plano TX

Missing, cracked, or curling shingles are the most common roof repair in Plano. Wind events routinely strip shingles from roofs across the area — particularly on older homes in Preston Meadow, Whiffletree, and Deerfield where 1990s-era 3-tab shingles are common. We match existing shingles as closely as possible, replace damaged sections, and re-nail loose areas that are vulnerable to future wind lift.

Flashing Repair Plano TX

Flashing failure is the second most common source of roof leaks in Plano. Metal flashing around chimneys, skylights, pipe penetrations, roof-to-wall transitions, and valleys deteriorates over time — especially in the heat and freeze cycles of North Texas. We remove failed flashing, properly prime and seal the substrate, and install new flashing with correct overlaps and sealant. This is a repair many contractors overlook or do inadequately.

Storm Damage Roof Repair Plano TX

Plano sits in Hail Alley — Collin County logged 21 major hail events in 2025 alone. Post-storm roof damage ranges from granule loss and bruised shingles to cracked decking and blown-off sections. We offer post-storm emergency response, full damage documentation for insurance, and repair or replacement depending on damage severity. Our written inspection reports are formatted for direct submission to insurance adjusters.

Residential Roof Repair Plano TX

We specialise in residential roofing for Plano homeowners. Plano's housing stock is predominantly single-family homes built between 1980 and 2005 — meaning most roofs are 20–45 years old and facing end-of-life issues compounded by hail and storm activity. Our inspectors are trained specifically to identify the failure modes common in this era of construction: granule loss patterns, flashing deterioration, ridge cap failure, and ventilation problems that accelerate shingle aging.

Roof Damage Repair Plano TX

Beyond weather-related damage, we handle structural repairs including rotted or damaged decking, sagging ridge lines, improperly installed previous repairs, and damage from tree branches or fallen debris. If your roof has been incorrectly repaired by a previous contractor, we assess the work and correct it properly — with documentation you can keep for insurance purposes.

Roof Repair Cost in Plano TX

Roof repair costs in Plano TX typically range from $300 to $1,500 for standard residential repairs. Here's a breakdown of what affects the price:

  • Minor shingle replacement (1–3 shingles): $300–$500
  • Small leak repair / flashing fix: $400–$700
  • Section repair (10–20 sq ft): $600–$1,200
  • Storm damage repair (insurance claim): $0 out of pocket beyond deductible
  • Full section replacement: $1,000–$2,500

If your damage was caused by hail, wind, or storm, your homeowner's insurance will likely cover the cost minus your deductible. We provide free insurance documentation as part of every storm damage inspection — no additional charge.

Roof Repair FAQs — Plano TX

How much does roof repair cost in Plano TX?

Most repairs range from $300 to $1,500. Storm and hail damage is typically covered by homeowner's insurance. We provide a free written estimate after every inspection so you know the cost before we start.

How quickly can you respond to a roof repair call in Plano?

Same-day response for most jobs. Emergency calls — active leaks, storm damage — are prioritised. We aim to be on-site within 1–2 hours for true emergencies across all Plano neighbourhoods.

Do I need a repair or full replacement?

We give you an honest answer. If your roof is under 15 years old with localised damage, repair is almost always the right call. If it's 20+ years old with widespread damage, replacement may be more cost-effective. We'll tell you which is true for your roof — not which generates the bigger job for us.

Can you repair my roof the same day you inspect it?

Yes, for most standard repairs. We carry common materials on our vehicles. More complex repairs requiring special-order materials may take 1–2 additional days.
