Hail Damage Roof Repair in Plano TX

Texas leads the US in annual hail events — in 2025, the state logged over 900 major hail events, and Plano and Collin County were directly in the path of several. Plano specifically recorded 21 major hail events in 2025. If your home was in the area during any of those events and hasn't had a professional roof inspection, there is a high probability of undiscovered damage.

Hail damage is often invisible from the ground and from the attic. It shows up as bruising and granule loss on the shingle surface — damage that only a trained inspector on the roof can assess accurately. Undiscovered hail damage accelerates shingle deterioration, voids manufacturer warranties, and can lead to leaks within 1–3 years if left unaddressed.

How Hail Damages Plano TX Roofs

Shingle Bruising

The most common and most misunderstood form of hail damage. When a hailstone impacts an asphalt shingle, it cracks the fiberglass mat beneath the surface granules — creating a weak point that's invisible to the eye but compromises the shingle's structural integrity. Over time, these bruised areas crack through under UV exposure and thermal cycling, creating leak points. Insurance adjusters look for bruising patterns to confirm hail events.

Granule Loss

Hail strips protective granules from shingle surfaces. These granules provide UV protection and fire resistance — without them, the asphalt base degrades rapidly. Check your gutters after a hail event: significant granule accumulation is a clear indicator that your roof has been impacted. On older shingles (15+ years), granule loss from a hail event often accelerates an already-deteriorating roof to the point of requiring replacement.

Cracked or Broken Shingles

Larger hailstones — golf ball size (1.75") and above, common in North Texas supercell storms — can crack shingles outright. These cracks are immediate leak points and are clearly visible in an inspection.

Metal Component Damage

Gutters, downspouts, flashing, vent caps, and AC condenser fins all show clear dent patterns from hail. These dents are important for insurance documentation — they provide objective evidence of hail size and intensity at your specific property.

How We Inspect for Hail Damage in Plano TX

Our hail damage inspection is comprehensive and specifically designed to produce documentation that insurance adjusters can act on:

  • Full roof walkthrough: We inspect every square foot of your roof surface, not just a spot check
  • Impact density mapping: We count and photograph impacts across test squares to establish hail density — typically measured per 10x10 ft area
  • Metal component photos: Gutters, flashing, and AC equipment documented to establish hail size
  • Interior check: Attic inspection for signs of water intrusion through hail-damaged areas
  • Written report: Full inspection report with photos and measurements in the format your insurer requires
  • Adjuster coordination: We can be on-site when your insurance adjuster visits to ensure all damage is identified

Hail Damage Repair vs. Replacement in Plano

Whether hail damage requires repair or full replacement depends primarily on:

  • Impact density: Industry standard is typically 8+ hits per 10x10 sq ft as the threshold for replacement eligibility
  • Roof age: On a 10-year-old roof, hail damage that doesn't breach the shingle surface may be repairable. On a 20-year-old roof with existing granule loss, hail impact often accelerates a roof that was already marginal into replacement territory
  • Insurance adjuster assessment: Ultimately the insurer makes the determination — our documentation supports the claim either way

Class 4 Impact-Resistant Shingles for Plano TX

When we replace hail-damaged roofs in Plano, we recommend upgrading to Class 4 impact-resistant shingles — the highest hail resistance rating available for asphalt shingles. Class 4 shingles are tested by UL 2218 to withstand 2-inch steel ball impacts without cracking, representing the most severe hail sizes that routinely affect North Texas.

Insurance premium benefit: Most Texas insurers, including State Farm, Allstate, and Farmers, offer premium discounts of 15–30% for Class 4 roofs. On a $2,500 annual premium, that's $375–$750 per year — the upgrade pays for itself within a few years while providing dramatically better hail protection for the life of the roof.

Hail Damage Insurance Claims in Plano TX

Most hail damage in Plano TX is covered by standard homeowner's insurance policies as a sudden weather event. The claims process:

  • We inspect and document all hail damage — free of charge
  • We submit a professional damage report to your insurer
  • Your insurer sends an adjuster — we can be present
  • Claim approved: repair or replacement proceeds at your insurance coverage minus deductible
  • If initial payout is insufficient: we provide supplemental documentation for additional damage

File promptly: Most Texas policies require you to file within 1 year of the hail event. Don't delay — call us immediately after any hail event in Plano, even if you don't see obvious damage from the ground.

Hail Damage Roof Repair FAQs

How do I know if my Plano TX roof has hail damage?

Most hail damage is invisible from the ground. Check your gutters for granule accumulation — that's a strong indicator. The reliable answer requires a professional inspection on the roof. Our inspection is free with no obligation.

Does insurance cover hail damage in Texas?

Yes — standard Texas homeowner policies cover sudden hail damage. You pay your deductible; insurance covers the rest. We document the damage and coordinate with your adjuster.

How long after a hail event can I file a claim?

Most Texas policies give you 1 year from the date of the storm. Don't wait — call us for a free inspection as soon as possible after any hail event.
