Storm Damage Roofing in Plano TX — What You Need to Know

Plano recorded 21 major hail events in 2025 alone. The DFW metroplex consistently ranks among the most storm-damaged regions in the country, with Collin County homeowners filing hundreds of millions in roof damage claims annually. If your roof was hit by a recent storm, there's a high probability you have damage — even if you can't see it from the ground.

Signs of Storm Damage on Your Roof

  • Dents or bruising on shingles (hail impacts)
  • Missing or cracked shingles after high winds
  • Granule loss — check your gutters after a storm
  • Dented or cracked metal flashing
  • Damaged gutters, fascia, or soffits
  • Interior ceiling stains or active leaks
  • Cracked or broken skylights

Insurance Claims for Storm Damage in Plano

Most Texas homeowner policies cover sudden storm damage including hail and wind. The key is documentation — insurers require detailed photos and measurements to process claims correctly. Our team knows exactly what documentation your adjuster needs and prepares it for you.

Important Texas law note: It is illegal for a roofing contractor to waive, rebate, or absorb your insurance deductible. Any contractor offering to do this is violating Texas Business & Commerce Code §27.02 and creating legal risk for you.

Storm Damage FAQs

How long do I have to file a storm damage claim in Texas?

Most Texas insurance policies require you to file a claim within 1 year of the storm event. Don't wait — contact us for a free inspection immediately after any major storm.

What if my insurance company denies the claim?

We provide detailed documentation to support your claim. If a claim is denied, we can provide additional evidence and you have the right to request a re-inspection or file an appraisal.
