Roof Repair FAQs — Plano TX Homeowners

How much does roof repair cost in Plano TX?

Most roof repairs in Plano range from $300 to $1,500 depending on the extent of damage. Minor fixes like a few missing shingles or a small leak around flashing cost less. Larger hail damage jobs — common after Collin County storm seasons — may be partially or fully covered by your homeowner's insurance. We give you a written estimate after every free inspection so you know the cost before any work starts.

Do you offer emergency roof repair in Plano?

Yes. We respond to emergency calls across Plano and surrounding areas seven days a week. If a storm has caused active leaking or exposed your roof structure, call (469) 613-0030 immediately — we can place emergency tarps to stop water intrusion while a full repair plan is prepared. We serve West Plano, East Plano, and all Collin County cities including Frisco, Allen, and McKinney.

How long does roof repair take in Plano TX?

Most standard repairs on Plano homes are completed within a single day. For larger jobs — full section replacements after hail damage, for example — it typically takes 1 to 2 days depending on roof size and weather. We always perform a final walkthrough with you before we leave and clean the site completely.

Will insurance cover my roof damage?

In most cases, yes — if the damage was caused by a sudden event like hail, wind, or a falling tree. Texas homeowners see significant hail damage claims each year, and most policies cover it. We help document the damage with detailed photos and coordinate with your adjuster. One important note: Texas law prohibits contractors from waiving your deductible — any contractor offering to do this is breaking state law and creating legal risk for you.

How do I know if I need a repair or full replacement?

A repair is usually the right call if your roof is under 15 years old and the damage is localised to one area. Replacement makes more sense if your roof is 20+ years old, the damage is widespread, or if repairs would cost more than 30% of a full replacement. Plano's housing stock leans heavily toward homes built in the 1980s and 1990s — which means many roofs in neighbourhoods like Preston Meadow, Whiffletree, and Deerfield are reaching replacement age right now. We give you an honest recommendation either way.

Do you help with roof insurance claims?

Yes — we handle the documentation side so you don't have to figure it out alone. We photograph and measure every area of damage, prepare a full written report in the format insurers require, and can be on-site when your adjuster comes out. We've helped Plano homeowners successfully navigate claims after major hail events including the June 2025 DFW storm that caused widespread damage across Collin County.
