Free Roof Inspection in Plano TX

A professional roof inspection is the only reliable way to know the true condition of your roof. Ground-level assessment — even with binoculars — misses the most important damage indicators: granule loss patterns, bruising from hail, flashing gaps, and decking deterioration that only become visible from the roof surface itself.

Our Plano roof inspectors are trained to assess every component of your roofing system and produce a written report that is useful for insurance claims, home sales, maintenance planning, or simply your own peace of mind. The inspection is free with no obligation to purchase any services.

What Our Plano TX Roof Inspection Covers

Shingle Condition Assessment

We examine every section of your roof surface for:

  • Granule loss — quantity, pattern, and distribution indicating age and hail history
  • Cracking, curling, and cupping — indicating age-related material failure
  • Missing shingles — from wind, improper nailing, or storm impact
  • Hail bruising — impact points indicating hail events that may qualify for insurance claims
  • Algae and moss growth — indicating moisture retention and shingle deterioration
  • Blistering — indicating improper ventilation or manufacturing defects

Flashing Inspection

Flashing failures account for a large percentage of roof leaks in Plano TX homes. We inspect:

  • Chimney flashing — step flashing, counter flashing, and saddle condition
  • Skylight flashing — most common source of indoor leaks in Plano homes
  • Vent pipe boots — rubber boots cracked from UV exposure
  • Valley flashing — open or closed valley condition and sealant integrity
  • Wall-to-roof transitions — step flashing and kick-out flashing condition
  • Ridge flashing — capping material condition and fastener security

Gutter and Drainage Assessment

Gutters are both a drainage system and a diagnostic tool. We check:

  • Gutter attachment and slope — proper drainage prevents fascia rot and foundation issues
  • Granule accumulation — amount and distribution indicating shingle condition
  • Downspout discharge — ensuring drainage away from foundation
  • Gutter condition — holes, separations, and overflow indicators
  • Hail denting on gutters — provides evidence of hail size for insurance

Attic and Ventilation Inspection

Proper attic ventilation is critical for roof longevity in North Texas — inadequate ventilation causes premature shingle aging, moisture damage, and ice dams. We check:

  • Ridge vent and soffit vent condition and clearance
  • Attic moisture indicators — staining, mould, or condensation
  • Insulation condition and any evidence of water intrusion
  • Decking condition visible from below — soft spots, staining, or previous leak trails

Structural Assessment

We look for indicators of structural issues including:

  • Sagging ridge lines or roof planes indicating rafter damage
  • Visible decking deterioration or soft spots underfoot
  • Evidence of previous structural repairs
  • Chimney condition — separation from house, tuckpointing condition

When to Get a Roof Inspection in Plano TX

  • After any hail or major storm event: Even if you see no obvious damage, hidden hail damage requires professional inspection to identify
  • When buying a home in Plano: A pre-purchase inspection can reveal conditions not visible in a standard home inspection
  • When selling a home in Plano: A pre-listing inspection lets you address issues before they become negotiating points
  • If your roof is 15+ years old: Annual inspections make sense at this age in Plano's climate
  • When you notice ceiling stains or attic moisture: Identifying the source before it worsens
  • Before filing an insurance claim: Our documentation maximises your claim potential

Roof Inspection FAQs — Plano TX

How long does a roof inspection take in Plano TX?

A thorough inspection typically takes 45–90 minutes depending on roof size and complexity. We provide a written report with photos the same day.

Do I need to be home for a roof inspection?

For the exterior and attic inspection we recommend being present — especially for the attic portion. For insurance or pre-sale purposes, your presence during the walkthrough is helpful.

Can I use your inspection report for an insurance claim?

Yes — our reports are formatted for direct submission to insurance adjusters and include the documentation format required by major Texas insurers.

What if no damage is found?

We tell you your roof is in good condition and provide recommendations for any maintenance items identified. No pressure, no fabricated findings.
