When Does Your Plano Roof Need Replacing?

Plano's housing stock is predominantly 1980s–2000s construction. If your home is in Preston Meadow, Whiffletree, Deerfield, Hunters Glen, or Los Rios, there's a strong chance your roof is approaching or has passed its intended lifespan. Add North Texas's brutal hail seasons — Plano recorded 21 major hail events in 2025 alone — and replacement becomes not just likely but urgent.

  • Roof is 20+ years old with original shingles
  • Multiple leaks across different sections
  • Widespread hail damage covering more than 30% of the roof
  • Significant granule loss exposing the shingle mat
  • Sagging, rot, or structural compromise
  • Repair cost exceeds 30% of replacement cost

Our Roof Replacement Process

  • Free inspection and estimate — full written quote with material specifications
  • Insurance coordination — we work with your adjuster for hail/storm claims
  • Full tear-off — all old shingles and damaged underlayment removed
  • New decking inspection — any rotted decking replaced before installation
  • Premium underlayment — synthetic felt for better moisture protection
  • Impact-resistant shingles — Class 4 rated for North Texas hail
  • New flashing — all chimneys, vents, valleys, and edges resealed
  • Ridge cap and ventilation — proper attic ventilation installed
  • Full cleanup — magnetic nail sweep, all debris removed
  • Final inspection + warranty — written documentation provided

Materials We Use

We install premium shingles rated for Collin County conditions. Our standard recommendation for Plano homes is a Class 4 impact-resistant shingle — the highest hail resistance rating available. Many Texas insurance companies offer premium discounts of 15–30% for Class 4 roofs, meaning the upgrade often pays for itself over time.

Roof Replacement Cost in Plano TX

A full roof replacement in Plano typically ranges from $8,000 to $18,000 depending on roof size, slope, materials selected, and any decking repairs needed. If your damage was caused by a hail or wind event, your insurance may cover most or all of the replacement cost minus your deductible.

Roof Replacement FAQs

How long does roof replacement take in Plano?

Most residential replacements in Plano are completed in 1–2 days. Larger homes or complex rooflines may take 3 days. We always complete a full cleanup before we leave.

Can I stay home during the replacement?

Yes. It will be noisy, but there is no reason to leave. We work around your schedule and keep access to all entrances clear.

Will insurance pay for my roof replacement?

If the replacement is due to hail or storm damage, your homeowner's insurance will likely cover the full cost minus your deductible. We help document and file the claim.
